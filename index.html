Approach

Most pitch decks don't fail in the design. They fail in the story.

Founders walk into the room with a deck that lists features instead of arguing a thesis. Sales teams open with a logo wall instead of the customer's problem. Executives speak to a strategy the audience hasn't agreed is worth solving yet.

Stealth Strategist starts before the slides. Every project begins with the question the deck has to answer in the first ninety seconds. The design earns its place by making that question — and the answer — impossible to ignore.

01

One argument per slide.

If a slide has two ideas, it has none. Every layout fights for the single thing the audience needs to take away.

02

Design as evidence.

Polish isn't decoration — it's a credibility signal investors and buyers read in the first three slides, before they hear a word.

03

Built for the room.

Every layout is designed for how it'll actually be presented — projected on a wall, screen-shared on Zoom, or sent cold to a partner's inbox.

How it Works

Four steps. No surprises.

A streamlined async process. You brief once, I deliver in days, you walk into the room ready.

01

Strategy intake.

Before any design, we map the audience, the ask, and the single argument the deck has to win. 30-min call or async brief — your choice. Most studios skip this. It's why most decks miss.

02

Narrative architecture.

I map the story arc and slide outline before any visual design begins. Structure is eighty percent of the work. The design only lands when the narrative already does.

03

Design and build.

Full design in PowerPoint, Keynote, or Google Slides — branded, polished, built to your audience. First draft in 48–72 hours. Every element earns its place.

04

Revise and deliver.

Two rounds of revisions included. Final file delivered fully editable plus PDF export — so you can update it yourself after delivery without starting over.
